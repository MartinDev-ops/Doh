Integrated Management of Childhood Illness (IMCI)

IMCI is an intervention strategy designed by the World Health Organisation and UNICEF to address common childhood illness namely Pneumonia, Diarrhoea, Malnutrition, Malaria and HIV/AIDS. The strategy offers a set of interventions that promote rapid recognition and effective treatment of common childhood illness in children less than 5 years of age.

All Primary Health Care facilities are implementing IMCI.

What Communities Should Know About the Care of Children

1. Feeding Your Baby

  • Breastfeed the baby (only breast milk) for the first 6 months of life. Breastfeed whenever the baby wants, at least 8 times in 24 hours.
  • At 6 months, start feeding the child freshly prepared nutritious food available at home and continue breastfeeding until the child is at least 2 years old.
  • For children 2 years and older: feed 5 times a dayâ€”3 meals and 2 nutritious snacks such as bread with peanut butter or margarine, fresh fruit, and full cream milk.
  • Feed using a spoon and plate.

2. Vitamins and Minerals

Give your child foods rich in Vitamin A, iron, and iodine. These include pawpaw, mangoes, peaches, apricot, pumpkin, butternut, carrot, spinach, fish, meat, chicken, and chicken liver.

  • Take your child for growth monitoring and promotion.
  • Provide ways for the child to see, feel, and move.
  • Use large colourful objects for your child to reach for and explore.
  • Give safe household items to handle, bang, and drop.
  • Provide stacking toys and containers to play with.
  • Help your child count, name, and compare things. Make simple toys.

4. Water and Sanitation

  • Dispose of faeces safely.
  • Wash hands with soap after changing nappies, before preparing meals, and before feeding children.
  • Use clean water for drinking.
  • Deworm your child every 6 months starting at 12 months of age.

5. Malaria

  • Know the signs: fever, fast breathing, headache, and sweating.
  • Take the child to the clinic immediately.
  • Allow indoor insecticide spraying.

6. Child Abuse

  • Protect children from abuse and neglect. Provide loving care.
  • If abuse occurs, comfort the child and take them to the clinic urgently.

7. Accidents at Home

  • Supervise children to prevent injury.
  • Keep dangerous items out of reach.
  • Keep children away from hazardous areas.
  • If injured, take the child to the clinic immediately.

8. HIV/AIDS

  • Prevent HIV in children by preventing infection in adults.
  • Know your status and practice safe sex.
  • Provide supervision, nutrition, and protection.
  • Support orphans and vulnerable children with care, education, shelter, and love.
  • Ensure antiretrovirals are taken correctly every day.

11. Danger Signs Requiring Urgent Clinic Visit

  • Child unable to drink or breastfeed
  • Child vomits everything
  • Convulsions
  • Child lethargic or unconscious

12. After Visiting the Clinic

  • Complete the full course of medicine even if the child seems better.
