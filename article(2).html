197 Patients Regain Sight at Mankweng Hospital

In a transformative initiative led by the Limpopo Department of Health, 197 patients underwent successful cataract surgeries at Mankweng Hospital, restoring their sight and improving their quality of life.

The campaign was launched by MEC Dr. Phophi Ramathuba on July 10, 2015, as part of the Nelson Mandela Month activities. The initiative aligns with the spirit of giving back and uplifting communities through meaningful healthcare interventions.

Dr. Ramathuba emphasized the life-changing impact of restoring vision, noting that sight enables individuals to live independently and participate fully in society. The surgeries were conducted under the Nelson Mandela Eye Care Month campaign, in partnership with the Rural Health Matters program.

Mankweng Hospital will continue to perform cataract surgeries and other essential procedures, ensuring ongoing support for patients in need. The department extended its gratitude to medical staff and partners for their dedication and commitment to public health.
