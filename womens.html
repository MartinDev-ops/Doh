Programme Objective: To improve the health status and quality of life of women and girl children by increasing access to their reproductive health rights.

1. Family Planning / Contraception Services

What is Family Planning? In simple terms, it refers to birth control or fertility control.

Objectives:

  • To reduce/prevent unwanted or unplanned pregnancies
  • To promote child spacing (number of years between pregnancies)
  • To empower mothers to decide how many children they want

Methods Available at Public Facilities:

  • Female and male condoms
  • Pill / oral contraceptives
  • Injectables (Nuristrate or Petogen)
  • Subdermal contraceptive implant (Implanon)
  • Loop / IUCD (intrauterine contraceptive device)
  • Female sterilization (Tubal Ligation)
  • Male sterilization (Vasectomy)
  • Emergency contraception (morning-after pill or IUCD) for women who had unexpected unprotected sex within 5 days

NB: The emphasis is on dual protection. STI and HIV screening is recommended.

2. Cervical Cancer Screening Services

Objectives:

  • To reduce the incidence of invasive cervical cancer through early detection and treatment
  • To reduce deaths associated with cervical cancer

Every woman aged 30 years and older should be screened using a Pap smear test. This test is available for free at all health facilities.

3. Breast Cancer Screening

Objectives:

  • To create awareness among the general population
  • To provide women with skills for Breast Self-Examination
  • To encourage early professional consultation when abnormalities are detected

Breast abnormalities to report:

  • Lump or thickening in the breast or armpit
  • Unusual increase in the size of one breast
  • Persistent discharges such as blood or pus
  • Scaly patches on the breast or nipple skin
  • Development of a dimple on the breast

4. Safe Termination of Pregnancy

The Termination of Pregnancy Act (Act No. 92 of 1996, as amended) gives women the right to choose whether to continue with a pregnancy.

Objectives:

  • To reduce maternal deaths caused by illegal unsafe abortions
  • To provide accessible, efficient, user-friendly safe termination services

NB: Termination of pregnancy is not a family planning method and may pose risks to a womanâ€™s long-term health.
