Adolescent and Youth Friendly Services (AYFS)

The Adolescent and Youth Friendly Services (AYFS) is a strategy that addresses the health needs and challenges of young people, especially at Primary Health Care (PHC) facilities. The strategy encourages youth to access services at PHC facilities and capacitate health professionals to adopt a positive attitude toward adolescents and youth. AYFS aims to reduce risky behaviours such as teenage pregnancy, substance abuse, STIs, HIV and AIDS.

Limpopo is one of the provinces with a high rate of teenage pregnancy. In 2012/13 the rate was 7.8%, and in 2013/14 it rose to 7.9%. One of the strategies to address this challenge is the implementation of Adolescent and Youth Friendly Services.

There are 117 PHC facilities implementing AYFS in Limpopo. Trained young people (Peer Educators) are placed in some clinics to assist adolescents and youth visiting the facilities.

The following information is provided to adolescents and youth visiting PHC facilities:

  • Know your body
  • Rights and Responsibilities for Adolescents and Youth
  • Benefits of abstinence
  • Information on HIV and AIDS
  • Medical Male Circumcision
  • Contraception
  • Integrated School Health Programme (ISHP)

Integrated School Health Programme (ISHP)

ISHP is a comprehensive package of services that addresses barriers to learning and other conditions contributing to morbidity and mortality among learners during childhood and adulthood.

Health promotion: All learners receive age-appropriate health promotion.

Services rendered during school screening include:

  • Vision
  • Hearing
  • Oral health
  • Height and weight
  • Nutritional assessment
  • Fine and motor assessment
  • Psychosocial services
  • Immunization

How the Parent or Legal Guardian Should Assist

  • Sign the consent form for the learner to be screened. Without it, the learner cannot be screened or receive services.
  • Ensure the referred learner visits the clinic or institution.
  • Note any allergies or relevant health information on the consent form to assist the nurse during screening.
